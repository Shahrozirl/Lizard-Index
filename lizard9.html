Leopard Gecko

The leopard gecko (*Eublepharis macularius*) is a small, nocturnal lizard native to arid regions of South Asia. Known for their spotted patterns and friendly nature, they are popular pets among reptile enthusiasts.

Diet and Feeding

Leopard geckos are insectivores, meaning they primarily eat live insects such as crickets, mealworms, and dubia roaches. A calcium and vitamin supplement is essential to prevent deficiencies. Unlike some reptiles, they do not consume plant matter, making a protein-rich diet crucial for their health.

Habitat and Environment

Native to rocky and arid regions of South Asia, leopard geckos require a warm, dry habitat. A terrarium with a temperature gradient, hiding spots, and a moist hide for shedding is necessary. Heating should be provided through an under-tank heat mat rather than overhead lamps, as geckos absorb heat from their bellies.

Behavior and Temperament

Leopard geckos are docile, easy to handle, and known for their distinctive behaviors, such as tail-waving and vocalizations. Unlike many other geckos, they lack sticky toe pads and cannot climb smooth surfaces. Being crepuscular, they are most active during dawn and dusk.

Lifespan and Health

With proper care, leopard geckos can live between 15 to 20 years in captivity. Common health issues include metabolic bone disease, impaction from loose substrate, and shedding problems. Regular feeding, proper supplementation, and a clean environment are key to their longevity.
