Bearded Dragon (Pogona vitticeps)

Difficulty: Intermediate

Bearded dragons (Pogona vitticeps) are one of the most popular pet lizards in the world, known for their calm personalities and unique appearance. Native to arid regions of Australia, these reptiles are named for the spiky "beard" under their chin, which can puff up and darken as a display. They are medium-sized, hardy, and enjoy interaction, making them a favorite for beginner and experienced reptile keepers alike.

Shopping List

  • 6’L x 2’w x 2’H reptile enclosure (front-opening with a mesh top)
  • Dual dome heat lamp with ceramic sockets
  • 90-100w PAR38 halogen flood bulb, x2
  • 50w ceramic heat emitter, x2
  • 5.5” dome heat lamp with ceramic sockets, x2
  • Large piece of flagstone or slate tile, for basking
  • Rheostat, x2
  • Digital thermometer/hygrometer device with probes
  • Infrared thermometer (“temperature gun”)
  • 34” T5 HO desert UVB bulb (Arcadia or Zoo Med)
  • 34” T5 HO light fixture with reflector (Arcadia or Vivarium Electronics)
  • 48-72” of ~6500K LED plant grow lamp OR 2 T5 HO fluorescent lamps
  • Surge protector with digital timer
  • 4-6 sq ft clean sand
  • Large reptile hideout/cave
  • Environmental enrichment items (logs, branches, plants, rocks, etc.)
  • Food and water dishes
  • Calcium powder supplement (no D3)
  • Multivitamin powder supplement (contains D3)
  • Soft-tipped feeding tweezers

Housing

The minimum enclosure size for a bearded dragon should be 6’L x 2’W x 2’H, front-opening with mesh ventilation. Larger is better. Only one dragon per enclosure to prevent fighting and injuries.

Lighting & UVB

Bearded dragons need full-spectrum lighting and UVB to thrive. Use a 34” T5 HO Arcadia 12% or Zoo Med Reptisun 10.0 UVB bulb. Mount over mesh (basking area 11-13” below) or inside (16-19” below).

Add 6500K LED plant grow lights for full-spectrum lighting to support activity and appetite.

Heating

Provide a basking area of 105-115°F and a cool side of 70-85°F using a cluster of 90-100w halogen bulbs. Use a temperature gun and probe thermometer for accurate readings. Turn off all heating at night.

Humidity

Maintain ambient humidity between 30-60% with a digital hygrometer placed on the cool side. Light misting 1-2x/week helps with hydration.

Substrate

Use packed fine sand or sandy soil at least 4” deep. Suitable options: Jurassic Desert Habitat, ReptiSand, Bio Dude Terra Sahara, or DIY mix of 50% play sand, 30% organic topsoil, and 20% Excavator Clay.

Décor

Use logs, flats, branches, plants, rocks, and textured backgrounds for enrichment. Fill the enclosure generously to encourage natural behavior and exercise.

Food

Bearded dragons are omnivores. Feed hatchlings insects 2x/day and veggies daily; juveniles 1x/day insects; adults 2-3x/week insects and daily veggies.

Insects: crickets, roaches, soldier fly larvae, mealworms, hornworms, etc.

Vegetables: collard greens, cactus, spring mix, arugula, carrot greens, dandelions. Fruit should be limited.

Dust food with calcium and multivitamin supplements as directed. Use Arcadia Earthpro or Repashy Calcium Plus LoD.
