Green Iguana (Iguana iguana)

Difficulty: Advanced

Green iguanas (Iguana iguana) are large, herbivorous lizards native to Central and South America. Known for their bright green scales, long tails, and iconic spines along their backs, they can grow up to 6-7 feet in length. Although stunning, green iguanas require a lot of space, specific environmental conditions, and daily interaction to thrive — making them best suited for experienced reptile keepers with the time and resources to meet their complex needs.

Shopping List

  • 8’L x 4’W x 6’H enclosure minimum
  • High-output UVB bulb and fixture (e.g. Arcadia T5 HO 12%)
  • Heat bulbs and dome fixtures
  • Thermostat and hygrometer
  • Infrared thermometer
  • Full-spectrum daylight bulb (6500K)
  • Large water dish or bathing area
  • Branches, platforms, and climbing areas
  • Leafy green food items
  • Calcium and multivitamin supplements

Housing

Green iguanas are large, arboreal lizards that need an extremely spacious enclosure. The minimum recommended size is 8 feet long by 4 feet wide by 6 feet tall, with plenty of branches and platforms for climbing. Enclosures should be built for high humidity and easy maintenance.

Lighting & UVB

Iguanas are diurnal and require high-intensity UVB light for 10-12 hours daily. Use a T5 HO UVB bulb (Arcadia 12% or Zoo Med Reptisun 10.0) mounted 12-18 inches above the basking area. Replace UVB bulbs every 12 months.

Heating

Provide a basking spot between 95-100°F, with an ambient temperature around 80-85°F and nighttime temperatures no lower than 75°F. Use halogen or incandescent bulbs for heat. Monitor temperatures with an infrared thermometer and digital probes.

Humidity

Green iguanas require 70-80% humidity. Mist the enclosure daily and use a large water basin to help maintain proper levels. Automatic misting systems and foggers can also help in larger setups.

Substrate

Suitable substrates include cypress mulch, coconut husk, or a bioactive setup with soil. Avoid loose substrates that can mold easily or cause impaction if ingested.

Décor

Provide plenty of sturdy branches, logs, and platforms for climbing and basking. Include foliage (live or artificial) for security. Green iguanas appreciate high vantage points.

Food

Green iguanas are strict herbivores. Their diet should consist of 80% leafy greens (collard greens, mustard greens, dandelion greens), 10% vegetables (squash, bell pepper), and 10% fruit (berries, mango, papaya). Avoid feeding animal protein or high-oxalate greens like spinach regularly.
